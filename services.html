What ObfusLabs actually offers right now

ObfusLabs is currently a one-person offensive security lab (hi, I’m Dan). This is not a big consultancy with a 24/7 on-call team. It’s me, hands-on, doing careful work with a small number of clients and a lot of time in the lab.

Realistic services I’m comfortable offering

  • Focused web app & API reviews — best-effort, scoped security testing for smaller apps and APIs (especially Python/Django backends and JavaScript/React frontends). You get clear write-ups, proof-of-concepts where appropriate, and practical remediation ideas.
  • Source code & configuration review — eyeballing security issues in application code and infra configs: things like auth logic, input handling, simple access control bugs, basic Docker/nginx/PostgreSQL misconfigurations, etc.
  • Threat-modeling & “how would you hack this?” sessions — 60–90 minute remote calls where we walk through your product, talk about likely attack paths, and sketch out priorities for hardening.
  • Second opinions on security reports — if another vendor gave you a report and you’re not sure what’s signal vs. noise, I can help you translate it into a prioritized, human explanation and a to-do list your team can actually act on.
  • Hands-on training / study sessions — informal, technical sessions for small teams (or individuals) where we walk through real attacks and lab environments together to build intuition.

What I am not (yet)

  • I don’t currently run a 24/7 SOC or incident response team.
  • I don’t take on massive, multi-month enterprise red-team campaigns.
  • I’m not a compliance shop for formal PCI/HIPAA/ISO sign-off.
  • If you need those, I can still help you think through what to ask for and how to evaluate vendors — but I won’t pretend to be something I’m not.

Why work with a tiny lab like this?

  • Direct founder time — you’re working with the person who’s actually doing the work, not an account manager.
  • Offense-first mindset — I spend a lot of my time in labs, CTFs, and web app exploit training and bring that into how I look at your systems.
  • Honest scope & expectations — if something is out of scope for me, I’ll say so instead of hand-waving.
  • Readable outputs — findings written in clear English, with enough technical detail that your engineers don’t have to guess.

Future idea: SOC-as-a-Service / SOC-for-hire

This section is intentionally aspirational. I do not offer a managed SOC or 24/7 monitoring today. What you’re reading here is the sketch of what I’d like ObfusLabs to grow into once there’s a larger team and more mature tooling.

Design goals (not live yet)

  • Blend real attacker tradecraft with day-to-day monitoring and detection.
  • Keep everything transparent and terminal-friendly instead of hiding behind busy dashboards.
  • Make detection tuning and threat hunting feel like a collaborative process, not a black box.

Rough shape of the future offering

  • Integrations — plugging into common EDR/SIEM/cloud/identity tools with read-only, least-privilege access and a heavy emphasis on responsible data handling.
  • Monitoring & investigation — alerts triaged by humans who understand how real attacks work, with clear, concise escalations back to your team.
  • Detection engineering — building and tuning rules so that they are actually useful, with examples and test cases you can run yourself.
  • IR support — helping you think through containment and remediation when things go wrong, with a focus on learning and improving, not blame.

Where things stand today

  • There is no staffed SOC or 24/7 pager for ObfusLabs yet.
  • There are no SLAs, no official runbooks you can buy off the shelf.
  • What exists is a growing body of lab work, playbooks, and prototypes that may eventually become the backbone of a real SOC-as-a-Service.

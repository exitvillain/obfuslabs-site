Future Idea: SOC-as-a-Service

This is not a real offering yet — it’s a direction I’d like ObfusLabs to grow into once there’s a bigger team, more tooling, and more experience behind it. I’m keeping this here so early supporters can see the long-term vision.

Why even think about a SOC?

A lot of companies want security visibility but can’t afford a giant SOC or massive vendor. Long-term, I’d love to build a small, attacker-aware SOC that’s transparent, lightweight, and human — not a black-box portal full of noise.

What a future SOC at ObfusLabs might look like

  • Human-first monitoring — analysts who understand attacker behavior.
  • Clear escalation — “here’s what happened, here’s what it means, here’s what to do.”
  • Detection tuning with an offensive mindset — fewer noisy alerts, more real signal.
  • Threat-hunting sessions focused on learning, not checkbox exercises.
  • Terminal-friendly tooling instead of flashy dashboards.

Obfuscope Console (concept)

One long-term tooling idea I keep coming back to is Obfuscope Console — a terminal-first command-line interface for security operations. It wouldn’t be a new SIEM or a flashy GUI. The goal would be to make it faster and clearer to operate the tools you already run (SIEM, EDR, cloud logs, and ticketing) without living inside ugly dashboards.

  • One place to query: search auth, endpoint, network, and cloud logs with a single command.
  • Readable timelines: compact story-mode output that reconstructs what happened.
  • Operator workflows: fast pivots, enrichment, and clean incident notes.
  • Tuning loop: treat alerts like code — promote signal, suppress noise.

What this is NOT (yet)

  • No 24/7 team.
  • No SLAs.
  • No enterprise onboarding process.
  • No compliance mapping or formal IR retainer.
  • No polished “product.”

Right now, this is purely a direction I’d like to grow into. If you’re excited about the idea — or want to help shape it — feel free to reach out.

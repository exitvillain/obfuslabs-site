Teaching Approach

A lot of students hit a wall when physics, chemistry, or calculus suddenly stop feeling like “plug-and-chug” and start looking like a foreign language. Symbols pile up, formulas feel random, and homework questions look nothing like the examples from class.

My job is to slow things down and make the ideas feel natural. If a formula is confusing, we break it down: where it comes from, what each symbol means, and how to recognize when to use it.

You don’t learn math or science by staring at notes—you learn by doing problems the right way. I guide practice so students build both skill and confidence. At first progress can feel slow, but once the fundamentals lock in, everything accelerates.
